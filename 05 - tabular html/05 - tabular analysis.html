What is this tool?

The IPHRA Tabular Analysis is an analytical platform that presents a multitude of quantitative data tables. It encompasses a wide range of indicators collected through the IPHRA assessment process, empowering users to examine and interpret complex datasets effectively. This tool is structured to support understanding the distribution of your data and support you writing your factsheets/outputs/reports, and create other visualizations.

What is in this tool?

Within this tool, users will find a wealth of information organized into distinct categories, each pertaining to critical aspects of humanitarian analysis:

Household (HH) Demographics

Examine the composition and distribution of populations within households.

Individual (IND) Demographics

Delve into detailed demographic breakdowns at the individual level.

Healthcare

Access data on healthcare availability, utilization, and specific health outcomes within communities on individual and household level.

WASH

Understand the access and quality of WASH facilities and practices.

Shelter

Review information on the types and adequacy of shelter among the assessed population.

Child Nutrition

Get insights into the nutritional status and risk factors affecting children.

Women

Focus on gender-specific data that highlights the situation of women in the context of healthcare, nutrition, and risk.

Death

Analyze rates and causes of death to gauge the health environment

Food Security and Livelihood (FSL)

Explore data on food availability, accessibility, and livelihood resources.

Mortality

Assess overall mortality rates to understand broader health trends.

MUAC/Nutrition

Evaluate nutritional status indicators, such as MUAC measurements, to determine the prevalence of malnutrition.

What else to know?

As you saw in the output folder, you will have another excel file outputed from the analysis script. The Excel file includes 4 sheets. The first 2 are all the tables that you see in the different sections of this HTML output. You can navigate to respective tables through the first sheet “Table of Contents”. The third sheet is nothing more than just the tables shown in the MUAC/Nutrition tab. The fourth tab is showing the Nutrition indices table disaggregated by sex and age. Please be aware of the footnote indication on the usage of the final percentages.
